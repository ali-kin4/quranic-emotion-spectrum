# Suggested Reviewers — *Religions* (MDPI)

The following three reviewers are proposed for double-blind peer review. Each works in
a field directly relevant to the manuscript (Qur'anic semantics, the cognitive
linguistics of Arabic, or conceptual metaphor in religious/scriptural language), each is
at an institution other than Urmia University, and none is a recent collaborator of the
authors.

> ⚠ **Verify current affiliation and email before submission.** Do not invent or guess
> contact addresses; confirm each reviewer's institutional email from their current
> faculty page or a recent publication immediately prior to entering it in the submission
> system.

---

## 1. Prof. Nicolai Sinai

- **Institution:** Faculty of Asian and Middle Eastern Studies, University of Oxford (UK).
- **Area:** Qur'anic studies; Qur'anic lexical semantics and the historical-critical
  analysis of key terms.
- **Why he fits:** Sinai's *Key Terms of the Qur'an: A Critical Dictionary* (Princeton,
  2023) and *The Qur'an: A Historical-Critical Introduction* (Edinburgh, 2017) are the
  state of the art in Qur'anic semantic-field analysis and chronology — both are engaged
  directly in the manuscript (semantic specialisation argument; Nöldekean diachronic
  control in the Supplementary Material). He is exceptionally well placed to assess the
  philological soundness of the fourteen-root field and the lexical-stage claims.

## 2. Prof. Zouheir Maalej

- **Institution:** Department of English, King Saud University, Riyadh (Saudi Arabia)
  (formerly University of Manouba, Tunisia).
- **Area:** Cognitive linguistics of Arabic; embodiment, conceptual metaphor, and emotion
  expressions in Arabic.
- **Why he fits:** Maalej is a leading authority on the conceptual-metaphor and
  embodiment analysis of Arabic emotion language (e.g. his work on figurative anger and
  fear expressions in Arabic, and the *Embodiment via Body Parts* volume). The
  manuscript's central CMT apparatus — the pressurised-container metaphor, the
  *qalb*/*ṣadr*/*nafs* locus distribution, and the *ghayẓ*–*kaẓm*–*tamayyuz* triad —
  falls squarely within his expertise, and he can rigorously test the universal-vs-
  culturally-elaborated claims.

## 3. Prof. Ning Yu

- **Institution:** Department of Communication Arts and Sciences, Pennsylvania State
  University (USA).
- **Area:** Conceptual metaphor theory and cross-linguistic/cross-cultural conceptual-
  isations of emotion (anger, the body, and cultural models).
- **Why he fits:** Yu's comparative work on metaphorical expressions of anger (notably
  the English–Chinese HEAT/CONTAINER analyses cited in the manuscript) is a foundational
  reference for cross-linguistic anger-metaphor scholarship. He is ideally positioned to
  evaluate the comparative six-tradition survey (Section 4.5) and the claim that Q. 67:8
  exemplifies a vessel-internal-failure construal distinct from the exit-typed English
  pattern.
