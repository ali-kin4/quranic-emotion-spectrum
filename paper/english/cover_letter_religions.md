Dear Editors,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript, "The Phenomenology of the Anger Spectrum in the Qur'an: A Semantic-Network Analysis of Displeasure, Inflammation, and Destructiveness Along an Action-Intensity Continuum," for consideration as an Article in *Religions*. The paper argues that fourteen Arabic roots in the Qur'an form a graded six-stage continuum of anger phenomena — from pre-anger displeasure through inner pressure, evaluative aversion, active anger, and compressed rage to behavioural outcomes — and substantiates that argument with a fully reproducible computational pipeline over the Quranic Arabic Corpus (Dukes 2011).

**Fit with *Religions*.** *Religions* publishes rigorous, methodologically transparent scholarship at the intersection of textual, linguistic, and cognitive approaches to religious traditions, and it actively welcomes Qur'anic studies that bring contemporary theoretical frameworks into dialogue with the classical exegetical and lexicographic heritage. Our study sits squarely within that remit. It integrates Toshihiko Izutsu's semantic-field method, Kennedy–Sassoon scalar semantics, and the Lakoff–Kövecses tradition of conceptual metaphor theory, and grounds every claim in the four authoritative Arabic lexica (*Maqāyīs*, *Mufradāt*, *Lisān*, *al-Taḥqīq*) and nine classical-to-modern commentaries. The work speaks to readers in Qur'anic studies, the cognitive linguistics of religious language, comparative emotion concepts across scriptural traditions, and Islamic psychology — constituencies well represented in the journal.

**Significance.** The study makes three contributions. First, a *methodological synthesis*: it applies a trifold Izutsu + scalar-semantics + conceptual-metaphor framework at corpus scale, with every figure, table, and statistic traceable to a versioned, openly released script. Second, a *novel empirical finding*: under uncertainty-aware reporting (a 10,000-permutation marginal-preserving null; Spearman, Mann–Kendall, and Cochran–Armitage trend tests; Holm–Bonferroni correction; baseline-sensitivity tabulation; bootstrap 95% confidence intervals on every centrality estimate), three roots survive Holm–Bonferroni correction (*karh*, *baghy*, *ghayẓ*), with *sakhaṭ* borderline at adjusted *p* = 0.050, and *baghy* emerges as the bridge node between the anger field and the wider moral-evaluation field. Third, a *bidirectional-causation reading of Stage 6*, validated through a κ = 0.79 *tafsīr*-coding audit of all 145 Stage-6 attestations against *al-Mīzān*, *al-Kashshāf*, *Majmaʿ al-Bayān*, and *al-Taḥrīr wa-l-Tanwīr* (anger-derived 26%, structurally derived 49%, joint 25%). The paper additionally frames Q. 67:8 of sūrat al-Mulk as an early and unusually transparent instance of the "container-collapse" failure mode in conceptual-metaphor theory, calibrated against a focused six-tradition philological survey (Akkadian, Biblical Hebrew, Homeric Greek, Vedic/Sanskrit, Classical Chinese, and pre-Islamic Jāhilī Arabic) that acknowledges Job 32:19 (*bāqaʿ*) as the one clear Northwest-Semitic precedent.
