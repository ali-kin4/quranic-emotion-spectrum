Dear Professor Abdel Haleem,

We are pleased to submit for consideration in *Journal of Qur'anic Studies* our manuscript, "The Phenomenology of the Anger Spectrum in the Qur'an: A Semantic-Network Analysis of Displeasure, Inflammation, and Destructiveness Along an Action-Intensity Continuum" (13,032 words; abstract 208 words). The paper argues that fourteen Arabic roots in the Qur'an form a graded six-stage continuum of anger phenomena -- from pre-anger displeasure through inner pressure, evaluative aversion, active anger, and compressed rage to behavioural outcomes -- and operationalises that argument under a fully reproducible computational pipeline released under MIT licence.

We believe the study addresses three concerns recurrent in JQS readership. First, it offers a **methodological synthesis** of Izutsu's semantic-field method, Kennedy and Sassoon's scalar semantics, and the Lakoff-Kovecses tradition of conceptual metaphor, applied at corpus scale to the Quranic Arabic Corpus (Dukes 2011) with every figure, table, and statistic traceable to a versioned script. Second, it reports a **novel empirical finding**: the exclusively Medinan distribution of *sakhat* (raw *p* = 0.005; the only spectrum root surviving Holm-Bonferroni correction over the family of fourteen per-root binomials at alpha = 0.05). Third, it advances a **bidirectional-causation reading of Stage 6**, validated through a kappa = 0.79 tafsir-coding audit of all 145 Stage-6 attestations against *al-Mizan*, *al-Kashshaf*, *Majma' al-Bayan*, and *al-Tahrir wa-l-Tanwir*: only 26% are anger-derived, 49% structurally derived (greed, *istikbar*, *'ulw fi al-ard*), and 25% joint -- reconciling a previously puzzling distributional pattern with the structurally driven character of *baghy*, *tughyan*, and *'utuww* in classical exegesis. The paper additionally proposes Q. 67:8 as an unusually transparent instance of the "container-collapse" failure mode in conceptual-metaphor theory, with explicit acknowledgement that no comprehensive cross-cultural philological survey of historical anger-vessel imagery has yet been conducted.

Methodologically, the paper aims to extend the linguistic-philological work of Sinai (2023) and the corpus tradition typified in JQS through state-of-the-art uncertainty-aware reporting (10,000-permutation marginal-preserving null; Spearman, Mann-Kendall, and Cochran-Armitage trend tests; Holm-Bonferroni FDR control; baseline-sensitivity tabulation; bootstrap 95% CIs on every centrality measure). It engages directly with the cognitive-semantic frameworks consolidated in Kovecses, Benczes and Szelid (2025, De Gruyter) and with recent corpus-Arabic NLP advances exemplified by Gaanoun and Alsuhaibani (2025, *Nature HSSC*).

In compliance with COPE and ICMJE secondary-publication guidance, we disclose that a substantively differentiated Persian companion paper is concurrently under consideration at *پژوهش‌های زبان‌شناختی قرآن* (NRGS, University of Isfahan), entitled «اوجِ طیفِ خشم در قرآن کریم: قرائتی تطبیقی-تفسیری از مرحله‌های فعّال، متراکم، و پیامدی» (~10,786 words). The two papers share only their dataset and methodological foundations: the JQS submission presents the comprehensive fourteen-root, six-stage continuum with full computational methodology, while the NRGS submission is a tafsir-comparative companion focused on Stages 4--6 with deep nine-tafsir analysis (Tabari, Qurtubi, Ibn Kathir, Zamakhshari, Tabarsi, Tabataba'i, Ibn 'Ashur, Sayyid Qutb, al-Manar) and engagement with Iranian-Islamic cognitive-semantic scholarship (Qa'iminiya, Pakatchi, Narimani, Qarizadeh). The papers cite each other and address different scholarly audiences; there is no content overlap beyond the shared corpus and pipeline.
