## A. Issues affecting BOTH the English and the Persian manuscripts (substantive / methodological)

**A1. Originality claim is overstated.** The paper repeatedly asserts (Abstract; ¦1.2 (i); ¦6.1) that the Izutsu + Kennedy¢Sassoon + Lakoff¢K÷vecses integration is "the first" in published Qur?anic studies. This is a strong claim that requires either (a) a systematic review of recent Persian and Arabic Qur?anic-linguistic scholarship (e.g. *Pizhuhish-ha-yi Zabanshinakhti-yi Qur?an*, *al-Dirasat al-Lughawiyya*, *Journal of Qur?anic Studies*) demonstrating the absence of such an integration, or (b) the more cautious wording "to our knowledge." The current text mixes both formulations inconsistently. The authors' own ¦1.3 lists Qa?iminiya (2011, 2014) as already integrating Izutsu + cognitive linguistics, which weakens the trifold-novelty claim.

**A2. Statistical interpretation problems.**
- The ?▓(1) = 1.55 result is presented (Abstract, ¦4, ¦5.4, ¦6.2) as *positive* evidence for "bidirectional causation" between Stages 1¢5 and Stage 6. This is a logical overreach: failure to reject the null of equal split is *absence of evidence of a difference*, **not evidence of equality** (and certainly not of any specific causal architecture). The text should be reformulated as "the data are *consistent with* a bidirectional reading; the test is underpowered to discriminate between a balanced-distribution model and competing hypotheses."
- The binomial test for *sakha?* (n = 4, p = 0.005 reported in ¦4 / Table 2) is dependent on a baseline of 0.74 Meccan ayat. The baseline derivation is described only in passing Ś please make explicit the denominator (suras vs. ayat vs. attestable contexts) and conduct a sensitivity analysis. With n = 4, even minor shifts in baseline (0.70 vs. 0.78) materially change the conclusion. The Abstract reports the same test with **p = 0.005** but Table 2 in the English version (p. 12) reports **p = 0.026** for the *exclusively Medinan sakha?*. This is an internal inconsistency.
- For *asaf* (5/0), the manuscript itself acknowledges in ¦4.4.3 that "binomial p = 0.16, suggestive but not decisive given the small sample" Ś but the Abstract and ¦1.2 still describe this as a "discovery" of an "exclusively-Meccan distribution" and a principal contribution. The framing should be downgraded accordingly.
- The headline result for the six-stage chi-squared (?▓(5) = 227.15, p < .001) is mathematically dominated by Stage 6 (n = 145) being four to fourteen times the size of Stages 3¢5. The test as run only confirms that the distribution is non-uniform; it does not validate the specific six-stage *ordering* the paper advances. A more discriminating test (e.g. monotonic-trend test, or comparison against a permutation null preserving total marginal counts) is needed to support the *continuum* claim.

**A3. Selection of "fourteen core roots" is partly post-hoc.** The criteria in ¦3.2 ("appearance as a focal reference in at least three of the four authoritative lexicaģ minimum corpus frequency of oneģ thematic membershipģ without significant overlap with adjacent fields") are not formally operationalised. *?ard* is admitted as a hapax legomenon "when the lexical and tafsir profile is sufficiently rich" Ś a subjective gate. The authors should publish the full filter list: which candidate roots (e.g. *q-h-r*, *Ü-n-?*, *?-d-w*, *?-r-b*, *q-?-w*, *g-l-?*, *?-n-f*) were *excluded* and why. As it stands, the fourteen-root inventory has the appearance of an ad-hoc choice whose statistical results are then reported as if they were predicted in advance.

**A4. Bidirectional-causation caveat is not yet operationalised.** ¦4.8.5 acknowledges that *baghy / ?ughyan / ?utuww* may arise from greed, *istikbar*, or structural arrogance rather than from acute anger. Yet the entire continuum framework, the chi-squared comparison, and the Gross-mapping table still treat them as Stage 6 *outcomes* of the anger spectrum. Either (i) drop them from the spectrum proper and reposition them as a *related but logically distinct* moral-evaluation field (which would also strengthen A2), or (ii) explicitly model the bidirectional relationship Ś e.g. by tagging each attestation as "anger-derived" vs. "structurally derived" on tafsir grounds and re-running the chi-squared on the anger-derived subset. The current half-measure undermines both the empirical and the theoretical contribution.

**A5. Tafsir engagement is selective and skewed.** The four tafsirs cited (al-?abarsi, al-Zamakhshari, al-?aba?aba?i, Ibn ?Ashur) are predominantly Twelver-Shi?i or Sunni-Ash?ari rationalist; the older philological tafsir tradition (al-?abari's *Jami? al-Bayan*, al-Qur?ubi, Ibn Kathir), the Mu?tazili tradition beyond al-Zamakhshari, the Sufi tafsirs (al-Qushayri, Ibn ?Arabi's school), and the modernist tafsirs (Sayyid Qu?b, ?Abduh-Ri?a's *al-Manar*) are absent. The ¦6.3 limitations paragraph acknowledges this in passing, but a reader cannot tell whether the lexical claims (e.g. that *ghay?* is exclusively predicated of unbelievers) survive beyond the four chosen tafsirs.

**A6. The claim that Q. 67:8 is "one of the most articulated linguistic instances of container collapse in any documented historical text" (Abstract; ¦1.2; ¦5.6; ¦6.1) is comparative and therefore requires comparative data.** No survey of pre-modern Greek (e.g. Homeric *kholos*, *menis*), Akkadian (*ezzu*), Sanskrit (*krodha*), or Hebrew (*?emŌ*, *?aph*) anger-vessel imagery is offered. Without this, the universalist claim is unsubstantiated. At minimum, the formulation should be softened to "an unusually transparent instance" rather than "the most articulated."

**A7. Missing literature.** The bibliography omits several works directly relevant to the paper's core claims:
- For Qur?anic emotion: el-Awa's *Textual Relations in the Qur?an* (2006); Sinai's *The Qur?an: A Historical-Critical Introduction* (2017) on Meccan/Medinan diachrony.
- For appraisal-anger: Frijda 1986; Roseman 1996.
- For Arabic anger metaphors beyond Maalej 2004: Yu 1995 (Chinese), Soriano 2003 (Spanish), Soriano & Maalej eds. 2014.
- For computational Qur?anic NLP: Atwell et al.; the QurAna and QurSim corpora.
- The reference list contains only **one Kennedy entry** (2007); the paper repeatedly cites "Kennedy 1999" but it is not in the bibliography.
- "Pakatchi" is named in ¦2.1 but not listed in the references.

**A8. The github repository link cannot be verified through the manuscript itself.** Both versions list `github.com/ali-kin4/quranic-emotion-spectrum` as the data/code archive, but the ¦3.3 description ("five-step pipelineģ master concordance CSVģ") does not specify the licences of the derivative outputs, the QAC version pinned, or how the morphological collapses were validated against a gold standard. For reproducibility, please add (i) a `requirements.txt` lockfile reference, (ii) the QAC SHA-256 used, and (iii) a confusion matrix between the pipeline's segment-to-root mapping and at least 200 manually-tagged tokens.

**A9. Translation-criticism Table 6 (English) is too small a sample to support ¦5.7's strong recommendation set.** Four verses ū five English translations is anecdotal. A reviewer would expect at least all 312 attestations ū the five translations, or Ś more practically Ś a stratified random sample of 30 verses with inter-rater coding.

**A10. Ethics, consent, and human-subjects clearance** are not discussed (corpus-only research is normally exempt, but a one-line statement is editorial standard).
